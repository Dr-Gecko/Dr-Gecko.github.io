Fun Activities for the Summer!


Summer vacation is coming up quick! Many of us have our plates full with school throughout the year, so the break, while relaxing, can seem boring if we aren’t sure how to fill our time. Lucky for you, I have created a list of possibilities for your summer!

  1. Volunteering is not only important for colleges, clubs, and jobs. Giving back to your community and society is sure to give you a feeling and experience like no other. Back before the electronic age, it was difficult to find opportunities for volunteers unless you really put yourself out there. Today, however, it can be very simple to find. Just pick up your computer (which I assume you’re reading this article on) and search “volunteer opportunities in my area.” Google will come up with plenty of locations, websites, and organizations open and willing to take in volunteers. If you live in a smaller community with less online presence, there’s nothing wrong with walking door to door downtown and asking if you can help in any way. In the long run, volunteering makes for better, more motivated individuals for not just our generation, but the generations to come.
  2. Camping is something that is very versatile and can accompany the needs of many. During the summer, especially, there are plenty of places that will work out well for a camping trip. The beach, the forest, a river spot, or even a yurt are all viable options during this season. Camping can also be used as a multipurpose vacation; not only do you get to spend time in the great outdoors, you can also hike, swim, create art, and spend time with family. Scheduling a camping trip is really as simple as planning one. In Oregon, there are hundreds of campgrounds scattered across the state that allow people to stay a few nights for free. There are others that meet specific qualifications, and some that require a fee. Just be sure to check online and call ahead of time to avoid being underprepared.
  3. These past few years especially, fires and smoke have filled our towns near the end of our summers; at this point, the fires are almost a season of their own. Throughout these times, it could seem as though there is nothing to do since outdoor activities are no longer an option. However, these days are the best opportunities for a scary movie marathon! Perhaps you’d enjoy catching up on the newer scary movies such as Us, It: Chapter Two, or A Quiet Place Part 2. Or maybe you’d rather re-watch the OG horror films like The Shining, Jaws, and Pet Sematary. Regardless of which movies you choose, a marathon is a fantastic way to spend a long period in doors… just don’t scare yourself too much.

Regardless of what you choose to do this summer, just remember to have fun and enjoy your time off in whatever way makes you happiest. Take your time, we can't wait to see you all again next fall!



Article by Jasmine White

Graduate REFLECTIONS



Graduation has finally come, and while I am happy to move forward with this next chapter in my life, I will greatly miss my classmates, teachers, and advisors. I’m so grateful for the experiences ORVA has given me and the opportunities I have been blessed with these past few years. To all my fellow graduates, congratulations! And to those who still have time left, your time will come sooner than you realize. If I could just pass on one piece of knowledge to those who will read this, just know that each decision you make will lead you to the next opportunity, the next experience, in your life. Continue to create your own path and do what makes you happiest, regardless of what others may think or say; after all, this is your life and only you can make it beautiful. Thank you to everyone who has helped to form my path and make my life beautiful. I will forever appreciate all of you. Good luck on your future endeavors, make yourselves proud!


-Jasmine White 2022 Graduate




I remember when I made the decision to join ORVA in 2016. It seemed like a hasty one, but I was fully confident and passionate to create a learning environment at home– it was a serious thing. The ways I was able to plan out my day on my own with no obstructions were really working out, so I stayed in ORVA for quite some time. Flash forward to September 2019 when I was starting the 9th grade, and I met some of the most hardworking, helpful teachers and staff members who helped me reach my academic goals such as taking college classes at 14. Even though we are all online schooled, and the pandemic shut down all ORventures for most of my high school experience, I made so many amazing friends and worked on school projects I was truly passionate about. My high school may have not had a football field or locker rooms, but it does have experiences that you’ll never forget (cheesy, I know). Thank you ORVA!


-Ambika Vyas 2022 Graduate
