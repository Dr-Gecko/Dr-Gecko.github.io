Short Story by Daiviani Bender

The Tale of Luke Finn


Magic has disappeared over time. Hunted by deadly creatures that take it for themselves, killing the user. The monsters were called azcras and they have hunted mages for thousands of years, which is the reason why a secret society in every state was built in hope that it would protect them.

The societies also exiled mages from the outside world, only allowed to leave with a special permit given by the society’s master. These permits were not just handed out or gotten easily, the master had to see one’s worth and decide for themselves if mages were worthy enough to leave.

However, some mages were born unaware of their powers, and most of those who were would be found by the azcras. And the majority of those who weren’t led normal lives. And others weren’t that lucky.

Luke lived in Brooklyn, New York with his single mother. He had no siblings and his father died when he was young. (Click here to continue reading. . . )

Fun World News

As we all know, a high abundance of our potatoes here in the United States come from Idaho, which contributes to making some of the things everyone loves—potato chips, Au Gratin, mashed potatoes, and everyone's favorite, French fries. How about a French fry scented perfume? On February 10th, 2022, the Idaho Potato Commission (IPC) released a one-of-a-kind perfume called Frites. Asking for only 1.84$ per bottle, Frites is made from locally sourced distilled potatoes mixed with essential oils to give you the nostalgic smell of a warm plate of French fries. President and CEO of IPC Jamey Hannigan said "Whether you're at a drive-through restaurant or dining in, it's near impossible to not grab a fry and take a bite before you dive into your meal. The smell is too good to resist. This perfume is a great gift for anyone who can't refuse a French fry," he said when asked about ICP’s newest release. If you wish to purchase this Perfume, it may be too late. As soon as Frites by Idaho was released on the ICP website, it sold out immediately. If you go to their Instagram page and enter the contest, you might be able to win some free bottles for yourself.

-Ava Wilderspin, 11th grade

Staff Writers Experience with EOP and Dual Credit

EOP by Jasmine White


High School at ORVA has been quite the whirlwind for me. I joined ORVA my freshman year, right before the strike of COVID-19. Honestly, I didn’t have much planned for the future other than a thought of a career in medicine. What I did know is that I didn’t want to spend an excess amount of time on things that I could get done during high school. A few months into my freshman year, I went to an informational class about the Expanded Options Program (EOP) here at ORVA. I automatically knew that I wanted to try out some courses at my local community college. I got in contact with my counselors who guided me throughout the placement testing and registration process. There was some adapting to do, but I got the hang of things relatively quickly. By spring term of 2020, I was enrolled in EOP at my local community college. I took a few classes pursuing the medical field when I decided that it may not be the best option for me personally. In turn, I started looking into psychology since it was still in the medical field and fit my interests more so than medicine in general.

With this newfound knowledge, I thought of my original reasoning behind starting in EOP: making sure I wasn’t wasting my time. I looked at my graduation plan and realized that I could fit the rest of my required credits into three years. I would be finished with high school one full year early. I spoke to my counselor as soon as I thought of the idea, and we formulated a plan that would create an early grad plan as easily as possible, which resulted in me taking between seven and eight classes for the second semester of sophomore year and all of my junior year. With everything set in place, I began my classes and am now on track to graduate in about four months. Saving time because EOP gave me both credit for college and high school this gave me the chance for early graduation at ORVA; being flexible and adaptable has completely changed my idea of life and helped me grow in so many ways. I didn’t believe at the beginning of my freshman year that I would have come this far and tried this hard in life. Never doubt your own ability to do so; roll with the punches, and try it out. The worst you can get is a no if you try, but if you don’t ever try, you have room for regret and wasted time.

-Jasmine White, 11th/12th



My Experience With Unique Educational Opportunities At ORVA: Dual Credit

by Ambika Vyas



If you didn’t know this about me already, I’m a senior graduating two years early and in the dual credit program. Many people wonder how much work I have to take on and how difficult it is, and to that, I say it is a LOT of work. Somedays, I spend the whole day enveloped in a textbook or doing problem sets. If you see yourself as an ambitious person when it comes to education, it is definitely worth it, though. Here are some quick tips and experiences I have for each opportunity:

Graduating early:

  1. A quick thing I should note is that I jumped one grade in middle school and one in high school. I didn’t jump 2 grades in high school because, at the time, it wasn’t possible.

  2. You may have to take extra classes within a semester and summer classes. Paired up with the dual credit program, you can take fun classes you enjoy or challenging ones.

  3. If you’re planning on going to college or getting a job, you might be the youngest there. It shocks some people when you say you’re doing this at 16, 17, but just know you’ve worked hard enough to get there.

  4. If you feel like you have everything in place and would be ok with one less year to retake exams, enjoying your favorite classes, extracurriculars, and school friendships, go for it. Sometimes people need more time, and that’s okay!


Dual credit:

  1. Read the guides for the specific program and Southwestern Community College Guide.

  2. Be ready for stricter deadlines–unlike ORVA, college professors do not give an entire semester to complete overdue work.

  3. Again, this depends on the class, but be prepared for many assigned textbook readings, especially with humanities/ liberal arts subjects. Take notes, highlight, and just do whatever works for you to process the important information.

  4. DC is a great opportunity if you would like to take advanced classes. If you’re like me and disappointed with the lack of AP classes at ORVA, maybe dual credit is the answer– it can provide a leg-up on college applications and your post-secondary education if you decide to continue.

  5. Be careful about scheduling! Don’t take on more credits than you can handle.

  6. Utilize the resources that ORVA and/or your partner college provides (tutoring, advising, etc).

  7. Here are all classes I’ve taken:

  • Spring 2020: College Survival and Success*
  • Summer 2020: College Algebra, Introduction to Game Design*
  • Summer 2021: College Composition
  • Fall 2021: Stress Management*, Intro to Assertiveness*
  • Winter 2021: Music Appreciation*, College Trigonometry (Continues until June)
  • Spring 2022 (planned): Technical Writing, Exploring Computer Science*

*anything with an atrix by it are EOP courses, not dual credit.


My favorite out of all of these is Introduction to Game Design.


If you have any questions about either of these opportunities, feel free to send your counselor an email. If you have questions for me, reach out through student connect.

Spring Break is Coming! Mark Your Calendars, and Fill Your Schedules Today!

School is out March 21st to March 25th for Spring Break! If your anything like me, you might be trying to find the best places to visit in Oregon so you aren’t spending the entirety of the break binge-watching that same season of Stranger Things that you’ve already seen 3-4 times now. Oregon is one of the most diverse places to travel around because of the varying climates, sights, and landscapes available all around us. Whether you prefer the coast, farmers’ markets, or hiking trails, deciding where to go and what to do can be such a nerve-racking decision because of Oregon’s diversity. Lucky for you, I picked out some of the best places to visit this spring break!

  1. Go to the coast for a day trip or full week! My personal favorite beach on the Southern Oregon coast is Sunset Beach in Coos Bay. There are some amazing hiking trails nearby at Sunset Bay State Park and the sights are wonderful! However, there are beautiful beaches everywhere in Oregon, such as Bandon, Winchester Bay, Cannon Beach, and so many more. You can find a beach with your specific preferences, whether that be camping sites or quick diners, online.
  2. Visit the Wooden Shoe Tulip Festival in Woodburn! This year will mark the 38th annual Tulip Festival in this beautiful town. There are millions of flowers to see and over 100 different activities to take part in. The festival opens on March 18th and ends on May 1st so right now is the perfect time to plan for your trip. For more information go to https://www.woodenshoe.com/events/tulip-fest/.
  3. A sight to see this break would be the Oregon Caves. Down in Southern Oregon, right outside of Cave Junction, sits the Oregon Caves National Monument and Preserve. There are many activities to take part in, including different types of tours of the caves, hiking trails, camping sites, and a dining hall. This trip is one everyone should take at least once in their lives and right now would be the perfect time to do so. Go pack your bags and go to https://www.nps.gov/orca/index.htm for more information on tours and directions.
  4. What’s better than some organic fresh fruits and veggies from all throughout Oregon in the spring? From jewelry, clothing, and art to homemade foods, farm fresh greens, and deep fried goodies, farmers’ markets carry it all. During a full week off, traveling around to different county farmers’ markets can be the perfect trip for all sorts of people. Each of the seven regions have outdoor markets of a sort, and each is unique on its own. The website linked below will share a list of different farmers’ markets organized based off of region and county for the best planning of travels around our beautiful state. https://traveloregon.com/things-to-do/eat-drink/farmers-markets/find-a-farmers-market-in-oregon/
  5. High school is the best time to look at all the options for our futures. What more efficient way can a teenager spend their break then traveling to different college campuses for tours and information about how your plans will be executed? Even just a day trip to different campuses might push you to begin a firm plan or fill out an application in the future. Just go to the website of the college you would like to visit and find information about scheduling and openings!
  • Written by Jasmine White 11/12th grader
