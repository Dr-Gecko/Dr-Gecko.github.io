In Remembrance of Finnian Lewis

Finnian Lewis, an ORVA 9th grader, passed away on April 20th, 2022. He was well known among students and teachers as he did well in school and was very active. “Finnian [. . .] from Bandon, Oregon, was a bright, polite, kind, respectful, and hardworking young man. He thrived in an online setting, rarely missed class, and always worked hard to get 100% on assignments. Finnian was described by others as genuine, full of ideas, and a positive example for others.” – ORVA staff


Some facts about Finnian:

He fully immersed himself in school and his assignments. It truly touched many different parts of his life. In the first semester of a Spanish class reflection, he had said ““It has been very fun and exciting experiencing these parts of a different culture. I've been exploring different parts of Hispanic pop culture or shows with a large number of Spanish speaking characters. That sort of thing. Once the world is safe to be in, I will immerse myself in more Hispanic culture, and try to talk to more Spanish speaking people. I know that knowing Spanish will help me academically and in job opportunities. Being bilingual in any language will open many more doors for me, but especially Spanish as it is useful to have someone at your place of work that can understand more people. It will likely also help the ease at which I can take vacations! I will be able to talk to much more people and be understood by more people. It will also be useful if I were to move to a place with a large Spanish speaking population such as L.A. As I could converse with people much better.” He had made a goal for health to eat 50% less junk food in his health class. From an analysis he had made on McDonald ads, he seemed to enjoy fast-food very much and knew he wanted to be healthier. Overall, he was a kind, sweet, thoughtful, and creative person who wanted to make something of his life. He also had a cute pug as his email profile picture.

You are missed, Finnian. Rest in peace.

Join DECA Today!

Oregon Virtual Academy’s DECA team is open for students to join! If you enjoy being a part of a team and fulfilling a leadership role, then this club could be a perfect match for you. According to a recent survey, our DECA members have been a part of the team for anywhere from half of a year to two years. There are many reasons our members so far have joined DECA, such as discovering a new passion, meeting new people, and focusing on their future. DECA is very guided and direct when it comes to campaigns and community service events, and it prepares many for their endeavors yet to come outside of high school in business of all sorts, such as hospitality, finance, marketing, and management. To become an emerging leader and/or entrepreneur, fill out the club survey and participation form and join our DECA team today!

Prom Night… A Bomb Night!

What can I even say… hands down this was one of the best ORventures I’ve been to.

Tacoma Street was a nightmare, but I eventually made it there at around 7:05 PM. The first person I saw was Mrs. Paulson! We met briefly and exchanged greetings. My parents and I made our way to the room where it all happened. We quickly muttered our goodbyes as it was a student-only event. The first person who recognized me was Brooklyn from the National Honor Society and her other friends, and we took photos at the photo booth. (Picture 1)


I spent a while searching for anyone else I recognized, but Ally Moore (also from NHS) found me first. I met her friends there as well and we caught some photos.(Picture 2)



(The photo booth has props???? Props???)

People started dancing to the “Cha-cha Slide”, and although I thought I wouldn’t dance, I ended up joining. (Picture 3)


I’m not sure if they played the song I requested (“Genesis” by Grimes), but they did play Marina and The Weeknd which are some of my favorite artists!

I met Paige (the Vice President of NHS) and we exchanged phone numbers. We’re going to be college buddies, and knowing that so early makes the future exciting. Trinity, the leader of Green Team, and my arts teacher Ms. Litner were also there. In total, I met SO many people from ORVA and NHS in person, some for the first time.


The most fun part? When I got pulled into a conga line..


To conclude, my classmates are the coolest people and I really hope we can connect after this school year. I’m going to miss ORVA!

Fun World News


April 27th, 2022 Georgia Tech students created a 4.2-mile hopscotch course that went through the Atlanta campus. The organization known as SEED, SEE(k) D(iscomfort) aimed to help students conquer their many limitations by creating out-of-the-box experiences. Physics major and creator of this operation Ashleigh Hennings said “It was one of those ideas that come to you and in hindsight, you can't remember how you got it," when asked about this crazy event.

CREATOR'S CORNER: A collection of Poems by Ambika Vyas

Beautiful Mornings

Beautiful mornings.

with my cup of tea,

I wondered and pondered into the sky,

as the sun clouds my eyes.

With my cup of tea,

I strolled around.

as the sun clouds my eyes,

the smell of the misty air…

I strolled around,

and gently opened the door.

the smell of the misty air…

Beautiful mornings.

Snowy Days

Skies that turn white

And clouds that turn pale,

As I take a bite

Of biscuits and a sip of ginger ale.

The children are pale

While playing today.

“Be careful of the hail!”

I shouted, hoping they’d obey.

I stroll outside, hoping

I do not slip.

By the trees, I’m eloping

With the mountains that chip.

“It’s time to welcome them and come on in.”

My hands were void of warmth,

But I’ve had fun and have been

Watching the cold north.
